Visual Identity Design

for Metropolitan Boston Housing Partnership (MBHP)
  • expanded visual identity system
  • brand graphic standards guide
  • sample design work

The MBHP team had little time to worry about the visual consistency of their many publications, with their resources stretched and their days primarily spent focusing on the community and the greater good.

Our primary goal: to expand MBHP’s visual identity, and create an easy-to-use guide and system of reusable elements.

Design at MBHP

The identity system needed to be easy to use for the small in-house marketing team, and the players on the ground: in the community and corporate board rooms.

Audiences

The system needed flexibility to support messaging for multiple demographics. For funders and decision-makers, a professional and contemporary feel, able to extend to handouts and powerpoint presentations. For participants and internal audiences, the system needed to a consistent feel and approachability.

Grid System

With an underlying grid structure, colors, patterns, and typefaces can easily become just meaningless decoration. The grid outlines how to consistently structure design elements throughout all MBHP’s collateral.

Typography

Due to the lack of in-house design resources, choosing typefaces that worked well and improved the brand, but were also readily available was a challenge. Fallback typefaces were also specified in the case of EXTREMELY limited resources and of course… email.

Colors

MBHP’s existing red (#A83338) worked and was in wide use. But they needed additional options to help customize designs while keeping a consistent look-and-feel; the palette was expanded to provide additional options for different audiences.

Photos & Patterns

Photos are an essential part of the identity, helping to convey the very human brand of MBHP. Photo treatments and various iconography-based patterns were introduced to create visual interest but also allow for a multitude of visual combinations all within the same visual style.
