Visual Identity Design

for Metropolitan Boston Housing Partnership (MBHP)

  • expanded visual identity system
  • brand graphic standards guide
  • sample design work

With their resources stretched and their days primarily spent focusing on the community and the greater good, the MBHP team had little time to spend worrying about the visual consistency of their many publications or internally-facing documents. They needed an approachable identity system that was not only easy to use for the small in-house marketing team, but for also for the players on the ground: in the community and corporate board rooms.

Our primary goal: to create an expanded visual identity, and an easy-to-use guide and system of reusable elements. Developing this system meant many things needed to be considered:

Audiences

The system needed to be flexible enough to support the messaging for many target demographics. For the funders and decision-makers, the system needed to be professional and contemporary, able to extend to handouts and powerpoint presentations. For the participants and internal audiences the system needed to have a consistent feel and approachability.

Grid System

Colors and patterns, and typefaces are just pretty (and occasionally meaningless) decorations without an underlying grid system to keep a consistent structure. The grid outlines how to quickly and efficiently structure design elements throughout all MBHP’s collateral, making it easy for those without prior experience to keep all documents visually homogenous.

Colors

MBHP’s existing red ( #XXXXXX) worked and was in wide use. But they needed additional options to help customize designs while keeping a consistent look-and-feel; so the palette was expanded to provide additional options for different audiences.

Typography

Due to the lack of in-house design resources, choosing typefaces that worked well and improved the brand, but were also readily available was a challenge. Fallback typefaces were also specified in the case of EXTREMELY limited resources and of course… email.
